## 1. Executive Summary

**SpazeAI** is an iOS-first generative AI photo & short-video app that lets users transform their selfies and personal photos into 74+ creative styles — from baby/aging filters to LinkedIn headshots, anime art, and cinematic car photography. Built on FAL.ai's diffusion infrastructure with FLUX Pulid for face-preserving generation and FLUX Kontext for instructed image editing.

**Positioning:** *"The fastest way to turn yourself (and your car) into anything you can imagine."*

**Differentiation vs. competitors:**
- **Breadth**: 74 styles across 7 categories (vs. Lensa's narrow Magic Avatars focus)
- **Quality**: FLUX Pulid identity preservation + Kontext for car / object editing (most apps still use SDXL or LoRA stacks)
- **Localization**: Turkish-first UX, ₺ pricing, local payment via Apple/Google
- **Social loop**: built-in feed + profiles for sharing creations (drives organic growth)
- **Speed-to-market**: launching with subscriptions + one-time packs from day one

**Revenue model:** Subscriptions (₺49–₺299/month) + one-time coin top-ups + 5 free coins for new users.

**12-month target:** 100,000 downloads, 5,000 paying subscribers, ₺3.6M ARR (~$106K).

### 3.2 Unit Economics — CRITICAL ANALYSIS

This is where most photo AI apps break. Let's model it carefully.

**Cost per generation by model (FAL.ai 2026 pricing):**

| Model | Cost (USD) | Cost (₺) | Used For |
|---|---|---|---|
| flux-pulid | ~$0.025 | ₺0.85 | Most face transforms (40+ styles) |
| flux-pro/kontext | ~$0.06 | ₺2.04 | Car styles (20 types) |
| sadtalker | ~$0.10 | ₺3.40 | Talking head video |
| live-portrait | ~$0.12 | ₺4.08 | Dance / aging videos |
| **Blended average** | **~$0.04** | **~₺1.36** | Across actual usage mix |

**Pro plan economics (the most popular tier):**

| Item | Value |
|---|---|
| Revenue | ₺149 / month |
| Apple cut (30%, or 15% after Y1) | -₺44.7 (Y1) / -₺22.4 (Y2+) |
| Net revenue | ₺104.3 (Y1) / ₺126.6 (Y2+) |
| Coins granted | 150 |
| Avg coin cost per generation | 3 coins |
| Avg generations per active user / month | ~50 (industry benchmark, ~33% utilization) |
| FAL cost @ ₺1.36/gen × 50 | -₺68 |
| Backend / R2 / infra per user | -₺3 |
| **Gross margin per Pro sub (Y1)** | **₺33.3 / month** |
| **Gross margin per Pro sub (Y2+)** | **₺55.6 / month** |

🟢 **Pro plan IS profitable at ~33% utilization** — but only because most users won't burn all 150 generations.

🔴 **Worst case (heavy users):** A Pro sub who maxes out 150 generations costs ₺204 in FAL fees → **net loss of ₺100/month**. This is why the **hard cap matters**.

**Key levers:**
1. **Hard cap = profit floor** — without it, heavy users would tank margins
2. **Annual plan**: lock in revenue, lower churn, smooths cash flow
3. **One-time packs**: highest margin (~70% gross) since no commitment
4. **Apple's small business 15%** kicks in after $1M annual revenue → margin doubles

### 3.3 Customer LTV Calculation

| Tier | ARPU/month | Avg lifetime (months) | LTV (gross) | LTV (net of FAL+Apple) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Starter (weekly) | ₺200 (4.3 weeks) | 1.5 | ₺300 | ₺95 |
| **Pro (monthly)** | **₺149** | **6** | **₺894** | **₺200** |
| Premium (monthly) | ₺299 | 8 | ₺2,392 | ₺580 |
| Annual | ₺208/mo | 12 | ₺2,499 | ₺820 |
| **Blended LTV** | — | — | **~₺1,100** | **~₺265** |

**Allowed CAC:** Net LTV / 3 = **₺265 / 3 ≈ ₺88 per paid user** to maintain a healthy 3:1 LTV:CAC ratio.

Given a 4% paid conversion rate from installs, that means:
- **₺88 / 0.04 = ₺2,200 max allowed CAC per install? No** — that's wrong direction.
- Correct: **CAC per install ≤ ₺88 × 0.04 = ₺3.50 per install**.
- At ₺3.50 CPI, you can profitably acquire users via paid ads.

**Reality check:** Turkey iOS install costs typically run ₺8–₺15 → **paid acquisition will be unprofitable on its own**. We need organic + viral channels to bring blended CPI under ₺4. Hence the marketing strategy below.

## 4. Marketing Strategy

### 4.1 Strategic Pillars

1. **ASO (App Store Optimization)** — free, compounds over time
2. **Viral/social hooks** — built-in feed turns users into marketers
3. **Influencer partnerships** — Turkish micro-influencers (₺1K–₺10K per post)
4. **Performance marketing** — TikTok + Meta ads, optimized for installs
5. **PR and community** — local tech press, Reddit, Twitter

Order matters. Months 1–3 focus on ASO and viral/organic. Don't burn paid ad budget until your funnel converts.

### 5.2 Critical Risks Deep Dive

#### Risk #1: FAL.ai Cost & Vendor Lock-in

**Why critical:** Your entire COGS line is one vendor. If FAL doubles prices, your Pro plan becomes a money loser overnight.

**Mitigation plan:**
1. Build a `lib/ai-providers.ts` abstraction with adapters for FAL, Replicate, Together.ai
2. Track per-model cost monthly; rotate to cheaper provider when delta > 30%
3. Negotiate volume pricing with FAL once you cross $5K/month spend
4. For high-volume models (flux-pulid), consider self-hosting on rental GPUs at $10K+/month spend

**Trigger:** Re-evaluate when monthly FAL bill exceeds $3K.

#### Risk #2: Apple Policy

**Why critical:** Apple has rejected entire categories of AI apps (NSFW filters, undress apps, deepfakes). One bad review by an Apple reviewer could de-list you.

**Mitigation plan:**
1. ✅ **Already built**: Black-image detection on FAL output
2. **Add**: Face detection on input — reject obvious NSFW input photos
3. **Add**: Report button on feed posts; auto-hide content with 3+ reports
4. **Document**: Clear content policy in ToS, in-app, and on App Store listing
5. **Test sandbox first**: Apple's review team is more lenient on apps with strict content controls
6. Have a **plan B distribution**: TestFlight beta + landing page that captures email if you ever get pulled

#### Risk #3: Currency Volatility (TRY ↔ USD)

**Why critical:** You charge in TRY but pay FAL in USD. A 20% TRY drop = 20% margin compression.

**Mitigation plan:**
1. Build currency volatility into your margin: assume **TRY drops 15% per year vs USD**
2. Quarterly price review — every Jan/Apr/Jul/Oct check FAL costs vs revenue per active user
3. **For Phase 2/3 (international markets):** price in USD/EUR — eliminates the FX risk on those revenues
4. **Hedging:** Once at $20K+/month USD costs, consider a simple TRY/USD forward contract via your bank

### 5.3 What Could Make This Fail

- **CAC > LTV**: Most likely failure mode. Mitigate via organic-first marketing.
- **Apple rejection**: Survivable but devastating in short term. Mitigate via strict content controls.
- **No virality**: If feed doesn't drive K-factor > 0.3, you depend on paid acquisition forever. Test feed engagement obsessively in Month 1.
- **Cloning by big players**: If PicsArt or Adobe ships these features, your differentiation evaporates. Move fast on the **car vertical** as a defensible niche.

### 5.4 What Could Make This Win Big

- **Viral car photo trend**: Car category is uncopied. If a TikTok trend takes off ("show your car as anything"), you could 10x in a month.
- **LinkedIn headshot wave**: Already a category. Position SpazeAI as the affordable Turkish alternative.
- **Wedding/influencer photo packs**: Bundle 50 generations as a one-time pack for influencers — high margin, high WTP.
- **Lensa-style viral moment**: A celebrity using your app could 100x downloads overnight.
